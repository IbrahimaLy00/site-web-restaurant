About Our Restaurant

We are proud to welcome you to our restaurant Djom Maro. Since 2015, we have been committed to providing an exceptional culinary experience.

Our cuisine is inspired by Senegalese culture, showcasing top-quality ingredients and boundless creativity. Our dedicated team of chefs and servers works tirelessly to offer you delicious dishes and exceptional service.

We are located in the heart of Dakar, Keretou District, a vibrant and dynamic place. Our restaurant is a meeting point for food enthusiasts, families, friends, and colleagues.

We look forward to welcoming you to Djom Maro and providing you with an unforgettable experience.

Our Specialties

At Djom Maro, we take pride in offering a diverse range of Senegalese specialties to tantalize your taste buds. Some of our featured dishes include:

  • Yassa Poulet - Marinated chicken with onions, mustard, and spices, often served with rice.
  • Mafé - A rich peanut stew made with top-quality meat and vegetables.
  • Dibi - Grilled lamb chops or skewers, served with a spicy sauce and mustard.
  • Ceebu Jen - A variation of Thieboudienne, a rice and fish dish with a tomato-based sauce.
  • Mloukhia - A traditional dish made from jute leaves, often served with rice or couscous and a sauce.
  • Pastels - Fried pastries filled with a mixture of fish, vegetables, and spices.
  • Bissap - A popular Senegalese drink made from hibiscus flowers, often sweetened with sugar and served cold.
  • Boulettes de Poisson- Fish balls made from ground fish mixed with herbs and spices, usually served as an appetizer.
  • Dakar Salad - A refreshing salad made with lettuce, tomatoes, cucumbers, and often served with a spicy dressing.
  • Cest bon - A dessert made from cassava and coconut, sweet and creamy.
  • Dakhine - A dish made from millet, often prepared as porridge.
  • Mbakhal - A Senegalese rice dish, typically served with fish or meat and a spicy sauce.
  • Domoda - A meat stew (usually beef or chicken) served with a peanut and vegetable sauce, oftenaccompanied by rice or couscous.
  • Thiébou Guinar - Another Senegalese rice dish with fish, often served with a spicy sauce.

These are just a few examples of the delightful dishes you can enjoy at Djom Maro. We invite you to savor these and many more when you dine with us.

Weekly Friday Evening Gathering to Celebrate the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH)

Join us for a special weekly gathering at our restaurant every Friday evening, starting at 5 PM. All Muslims are cordially invited to come together to glorify and honor the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).

This event will include a spiritual and religious dimension, and we've planned the following activities to create a meaningful and reflective atmosphere:

  • Religious Talks or Lectures: Renowned religious speakers will deliver talks and lectures on the life, teachings, and virtues of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).
  • Quran Recitations: Experience Quranic recitations to infuse the environment with spirituality and serenity.
  • Nasheeds (Religious Chants): Enjoy performances of nasheeds (religious chants) in honor of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).
  • Hadith Discussions: Engage in discussions on Hadiths, the sayings and actions of the Prophet, to gain a deeper understanding of his life and teachings.
  • Community Meal: Share a communal meal to celebrate unity and brotherhood. Our dishes are prepared in accordance with halal culinary principles.
  • Group Prayer: Participate in a congregational Friday prayer (Salat al-Jumu'ah) for those who wish to join.
  • Learning Workshops: Attend workshops on faith and spirituality-related topics, such as ethics, morality, and charity.
  • Distribution of Religious Materials: Receive brochures or religious books to deepen your understanding of Islam and the life of the Prophet.
  • Proper Decoration: Adorn the space with appropriate visual elements, including Quranic verses and calligraphy honoring the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).
  • Warm Welcome: Ensure all participants feel welcome and valued, fostering a warm and inclusive atmosphere.

We prioritize respecting religious sensitivities and ensuring that all aspects of the event are in line with Islamic beliefs and practices. Promoting unity, peace, and understanding is our noble goal for this gathering.
